A Comparison of Minimum Wage Across Three States

None of these three states have increased their minimum wage by more than a dollar since 2022.

By Rachel Musselwhite

As someone who has lived in a variety of states, I've found it jarring to move to a new place and be confronted by a different cost of living - and a different minimum wage.

For purposes of this project, I compared three states - Alaska, California and Florida - and their minimum wages over the past three years.

California has the highest minimum wage in the nation. And depending on which city you live in, it could be even higher than the state's wage of $16.00. For example, San Francisco - where I previously lived - has a minimum wage of $18.07

Although Florida's minimum wage is less than California's, it will continue to increase over the next few years. According to the Department of Labor, the minimum wage will increase by one dollar every September 30th. This will stop when the minimum wage reaches reaches $15.00, which will happen September 30, 2026.

Alaska does not have any planned wage increases in its future.
